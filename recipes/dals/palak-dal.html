Palak Dal

Step 1: For the Cooker
  • 0.5 Cup Toor Dal
  • 2 Cups Fresh Palak
  • 2 Tomatoes (Medium)
  • 2 Green Chilies
  • 1 Small pc Tamarind
  • 0.5 tsp Turmeric & Salt
Step 2: For the Tadka Pan
  • 1 Onion (Small)
  • 4 Cloves Garlic
  • 2 Dry Red Chilies
  • 1 tsp Mustard & Cumin
  • 8 Curry Leaves
Kitchen Tools
  • Pressure Cooker
  • Tadka Pan
  • Wooden Spoon
  • Whisk / Masher
00:00

Tap task to start

💡 Beginner Tip: Mashing the cooked dal releases starch, making the curry creamy without adding cream!
Rinse Dal

Wash ½ cup toor dal 3 times until water is clear to remove excess starch.

1:00 MIN
Pressure Cook

Add dal, tomatoes, palak, turmeric, salt, tamarind, and 2 glasses of water. High flame for 3 whistles, then simmer 5 mins.

15:00 MINS
Release & Mash

Turn off flame and let cooker cool naturally. Mash the dal lightly with a whisk once opened.

5:00 MINS
💡 Doneness Check: Fry garlic until it smells nutty and looks light golden. If it turns black, it becomes bitter!
Fry Garlic & Red Chilies

Heat oil in tadka pan. Fry crushed garlic and 2 dry red chilies until golden and fragrant.

0:40 SEC
Temper Spices & Onion

Add cumin, mustard, curry leaves, and onion. Sauté until onions are translucent and soft.

2:00 MINS
💡 Flavor Secret: Pouring the hot tadka into the dal and immediately covering it traps the smoky aroma!
Combine & Simmer

Pour the hot tadka over the mashed dal. Simmer together for 5 mins to blend flavors.

5:00 MINS

Enjoy your Palak Dal! 🍽️
